Lunar Phases

Artist Giovanni Battista Riccioli
Date April 22, 1650
Medium Copper engraving
Dimensions 32 × 24 cm

Description

This elegant astronomical diagram illustrates the phases of the Moon as observed from Earth throughout its monthly orbit. Created during the height of the Scientific Revolution, this work represents a significant advancement in astronomical understanding. The diagram demonstrates how the Moon's appearance changes based on its position relative to the Sun and Earth, showing the progression from new moon through the waxing and waning phases to full moon and back again. The precision and clarity of this illustration made it an essential teaching tool in astronomical education for centuries.

Historical Context

Giovanni Battista Riccioli was an Italian astronomer and Jesuit priest who made significant contributions to lunar cartography. His detailed observations and systematic approach to documenting celestial phenomena helped establish the methodologies that would guide astronomical research for generations. This particular diagram appeared in his influential work "Almagestum Novum," which attempted to reconcile traditional astronomical models with emerging telescopic observations.
